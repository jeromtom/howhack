A guide to your first hackathon.
A hackathon is a social coding event that brings computer programmers, UI/UX designers, and other interested people together to improve upon or build a new software program. The word hackathon is a portmanteau of the words hacker, which means clever programmer, and marathon, an event marked by endurance.
Before

Finding hackathons:

Check the clubs in your college and ask seniors. They regularly organize hackathons, help up skill and form teams to participate in hackathons outside college.

 

Online resources like Major League Hacking, Devfolio, Devpost, HackerEarth, etc. list and manage offline and online hackathons and even help in team formation.

It is better to form a team to participate in hackathons, so bring along someone you know and can complement your skill set. Most hackathons nowadays also have a team forming section, you might be able to find your team there.

 

If you are a designer/frontend search for a developer/back-end, and vice versa.

 

Similarly, if you are a beginner, tag along with someone more experienced or with another beginner to learn together.

After

Congratulations on your first hackathon. It is okay if you did not win any prizes but you never lose, you always learn.

 

Stay connected to the new people that you met, follow their socials. Invite them to future hackathons that you come across and team up if suitable.

 

By attending hackathons you will also build a network of designers and developers that you can access to solve doubts, build projects and even get referrals for jobs and internships in the future.

Keep an eye on the upcoming hackathons and market hem in your calendar.

 
During

Peacefully read through all the themes and/or problem statements and bounty prizes.

 

Select a theme/problem statement that you/ your team is interested in and can solve during the timeline.

 

If anyone in your team has done similar past projects, it will turn our to be very helpful during the hackathon,

 

Try to incorporate the condition specified in different bounty tracks with sponsored prices to increase the chances of getting a bounty.

 

The goal is to make an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) that will solve to core problem. More features can be added if you have time left.

Hackathons might also have pitching or presentation live or in the form of a video submission. This another opportunity for your project to shine. Check out Loom, it is useful screen recording software and you an also use Canva for making presentations.

 

It is a competion with time constraints but it is for you to have fun not to tire you. If you feel your productivity levels falling, take a nap or take a walk.

 

Engage in side games organized or orgaise one yourself, your fellow attendees will love it. The point of going to hackathons is also to network, you will make new friend that can help you grow as a developer/designer.
